# Persuasive Infographic – Sexual Assault Awareness in Canada  

## Overview  
This project presents a persuasive infographic focused on sexual assault awareness in Canada, developed during April, which is recognized as Sexual Assault Awareness Month.  

The infographic aims to highlight the gap between reported and unreported cases, identify high risk groups, and encourage awareness, conversation, and support for survivors.

---

## Objective  
- Communicate a clear and impactful message using data  
- Raise awareness about **underreporting of sexual assault**  
- Identify vulnerable populations through visual storytelling  
- Design an infographic accessible to a general audience 

---

## Key Message  
Sexual assault is significantly underreported in Canada, with only **6% of cases being reported**.  

The infographic emphasizes that:
- Many cases remain invisible in official statistics  
- Certain groups are disproportionately affected  
- Awareness and open conversation are essential steps toward change  

---

## Key Insights  
- Only a small percentage of sexual assault cases are officially reported  
- Higher risk groups include:  
  - Young women  
  - Indigenous women  
  - Women with disabilities  

The goal is to move beyond statistics and encourage **empathy, awareness, and action**.

## Target Audience  
- General public  
- Individuals with limited data literacy  
- People unaware of the scale of underreporting  

The design prioritizes clarity, simplicity, and emotional engagement over technical complexity.

---

## Design Principles Applied  
- Clear visual hierarchy and structured layout  
- Minimal text with emphasis on visuals   
- Consistent color scheme and typography  
- Use of whitespace for readability  
- Simple and intuitive charts for accessibility  

---

## Reflection  
This project demonstrates how data visualization can be used as a tool for public awareness and social impact.  

By focusing on a sensitive and often underreported issue, the infographic aims to:
- Encourage conversations  
- Support survivors  
- Increase public understanding  

Ultimately, the project highlights the importance of using data not only to inform, but also to advocate and create change.
